About the Accessibility Score

What the Accessibility Score Measures

Each classified bus stop is rated on four recorded access factors:

  • Boarding Space — whether there is enough clear space to get on and off the bus.
  • Road Slope — how steep the road is at the stop.
  • Sideways Tilt — how much the boarding area slopes across.
  • Tactile Paving — textured ground that helps blind or low-vision passengers find a safe boarding point.

How the Accessibility Score is Calculated

Each classified bus stop was converted into an accessibility score out of 7 to make the data easier to compare across suburbs.

Boarding space, road slope, and sideways tilt were scored as Easy = 2, Medium = 1, Hard = 0, while tactile paving was scored as Yes = 1, No = 0.

A higher score means the stop is likely to be easier to approach, wait at, and board from — with more clear space, gentler slopes, less sideways tilt, and tactile paving to support safer access.
